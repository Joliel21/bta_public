# Housecleaning

**Tips and Tricks I Learned Along the Way**

By Jolie Lizana

Publication date: September 16, 2025

---

Safety note: These tips are based on personal experience and are for general support only. Pulmonary hypertension (PH), dizziness, fainting, shortness of breath, oxygen needs, and activity limits should be discussed with your doctor or PH care team. If an activity makes you dizzy, faint, short of breath, causes chest pain, or feels unsafe, stop and seek medical guidance.

Household “duties” are tricky topics to give tips on because we can be at different points in our journey, and our limitations vary greatly. I started getting very sick while going to college and driving a minimum of 5 1/2 hours a day. I had blood clots in both lungs and heart failure. All I knew was that I was extremely sick and unable to do things. My body wouldn’t let me. I was shutting down.

Finally, sadly, after my crash, I was diagnosed with the aforementioned and PAH (PH). I was on IV medication that was saving my life, literally. The sickness and pain this medication caused were awful. I was extremely sick!

So for the first couple of years, I was too ill to do just about any cleaning or cooking, had trouble walking, and couldn’t get out of bed easily; I couldn’t walk more than 5 feet. I crashed hard! Yet, I still didn’t listen to my body enough. I pushed too much.

Please listen to your body.

Question yourself before you:

- drive
- bend over
- lift your hands above your head
- go upstairs
- walk up inclines
- twist your body
- do anything else that makes you dizzy, winded, weak, or unsafe

I’ve passed out bending over to do laundry a few times. I’ve passed out several times, too many times, from not listening to my body and pushing myself. I am lucky that I did not seriously hurt myself. Please learn from others’ (okay, well, my) mistakes.

## Dusting

Kneel, sit on a step stool, or sit on the floor, wherever you can, to minimize frequent bending over. Use caution when reaching above your head. Dust lightly. Scrubbing can cause breathlessness.

## Kitchen

Hand washing is simple if you have the strength and not many dishes to wash. I advise you not to scrub if it causes you to lose your breath. Allow things to soak in hot water with dish soap overnight if necessary. If you use the dishwasher, remember that people with PH may become dizzy when bending over. When you lean over to empty the dishwasher, brace yourself by holding the counter. Once you are in a position to get dishes, stay in this position. Take the dishes from the bottom out all at once, without standing up or bending any further. Go slow. Once they are all on the counter, stand up carefully and make sure you are not dizzy before putting them away. Anything that goes overhead should be done sparingly in between putting up easy-to-reach dishes. Continue for the top shelf. Go slow; listen to your body. Your doctor or PH care team may advise you to avoid bending over. Ask what is safe for your situation.

## Mopping Solution

I use a bare-floor vacuum with a wet-spray and cleaning pad all-in-one. It’s wonderful. I have a family that does deep mopping and vacuuming as needed. Please read “Sweeping.”

## Sweeping

Sweeping is not something I can do without tiring myself out quickly or ending up in severe pain from holding my neck down for so long. Moving our arms in a sweeping motion around our bodies causes our chests to rise and fall, thereby altering our breathing. These motions may be difficult for some people with PH. I use a bare-floor vacuum that features a wet spray and a cleaning pad, all in one. It’s wonderful. I have a family that does deep mopping and vacuuming as needed.

## Vacuuming Solution

Be sure to buy a lightweight vacuum (vac). Not one that claims to be lightweight. Always check the weight listed in the description. Light to one manufacturer is obviously different from that of others (I won’t state which – just be sure to check).

If the vacuum is difficult to push, adjust the carpet setting. Sit down on the floor or have someone assist you if you feel you shouldn’t bend over. If you are having to push hard and it’s altering your breathing, stop. I found it easiest to move forward by getting behind it with both hands on the handle, then leaning forward using my weight, rather than my arms. Pushing our arms forward alters our chest position, which in turn affects our breathing. To return the vacuum to its original position, try facing in the opposite direction. Grab the handle with both hands, keeping them behind your back. Lock your hands on and walk forward, dragging it behind you. Just do what you can to keep your chest and your core straight.

## Wiping Counters & other items

Wiping surfaces may be an issue if you need to bend down to do it. I will sometimes kneel or sit on a step stool/floor, whatever can keep me from leaning downward. Try not to scrub. Allow cleaning products time to help remove grime. Use caution when reaching overhead. As we change our chest and ribcage, we alter our breathing.

## Laundry

When doing laundry, try to sit on a stool or the ground to move wet clothes to the dryer. If you have an upright opening, place a short laundry basket or similar item on the edge of the washer. Brace yourself with your hands and bend into a position where you can reach the clothes and put them into the basket without raising or tilting. Put all of the wet clothes into the basket. Slowly raise, again bracing and going as slowly as you need to. Once you can pick up the basket, carefully lower it to the ground. Put the wet clothes in the dryer by kneeling or sitting on something. Bracing here and leaning could be dangerous because the dryer barrel is much lower than the washer barrel.

Remember to keep your core and chest straight and maintain that position while doing the laundry. Go at what pace you are comfortable with. Listen to your mind and body. Pushing yourself may increase dizziness, shortness of breath, or fainting risk. I have passed out more than once doing laundry. Please learn from my mistakes.
