# Doctor’s Visits & Daily Health

**Tips and tricks to help make the most of your appointments.**

By Jolie Lizana

Publication date: September 16, 2025

---

Safety note: These tips are based on personal experience and are for general support only. Pulmonary hypertension (PH), heart failure, fluid limits, sodium limits, weight changes, symptoms, medications, and test results should be discussed with your doctor or PH care team. If you have sudden weight gain, worsening shortness of breath, chest pain, fainting, swelling, or symptoms that feel urgent, contact your care team or seek emergency care.

I suggest getting a small binder or notebook for doctor’s visits. I like a binder because I can rearrange pages and organize things more easily. In the binder, write down any questions that you or your family have. Bring those questions to your next doctor’s visit and ask them. If you feel like it is a silly question, stop thinking that. Any question you have about your health deserves an answer.

Be your own best advocate. Learn about your body, your diagnosis, your medications, your symptoms, and what to expect. If you have many questions, tell your doctor and ask who else on the care team can help answer them. Doctors have busy schedules, but that does not mean your questions should go unanswered. PH nurses, PH coordinators, pharmacists, social workers, and other care-team members may be able to help or point you in the right direction.

This binder can hold answers to your questions, appointment notes with dates, medication changes, test results, care team instructions, and anything you want to remember. Dating your notes can help because treatment plans, care pathways, symptoms, and circumstances can change over time.

The binder can also be a helpful place to track fluid intake, sodium intake, daily weight, symptoms, medication changes, refill dates, appointment dates, test dates, and test results. Ask your doctor or PH care team what they want you to track and what changes should prompt a call. For example, some people are told to call their doctor if they gain several pounds quickly, but your personal instructions may be different. Make sure your care team gives you clear guidance about fluid limits, sodium limits, weight changes, and when to contact them.
